So Say We All

April 18, 2015

This week has been spent crushing through Ruby classes and the beginnings of object-oriented programming. Classes are similar things that do similar stuff. They have attributes that are similar and they take similar actions. For example, a human being has more in common with a cheetah than it does, say, a radish. Human beings and cheetahs both move around on things called legs. Radishes are known to be rather stationary. However, one might take a further step back and say that human, cheetahs and radishes are all living things, and have more in common with each other than they do with say...a drill press. Where to draw lines and how to create classes seems to me to be one of the most fundamental skills in Ruby.
